About

Hi! Thank you for visiting! My name is Ben Reilly and I am a software engineer who mostly does full-stack web development type-stuff.

I have many interests but my current hobby is machine learning, especially natural language processing and image translation. I like machine learning because it reminds me of the magical, uncanny, and deeply weird phenomena we can find in computers and the internet.

I like to see the internet, even all of the cold, mechanical, commercial, and horrifying parts of it, as a massive communal art project. It's a kind of mirror, a simlacrum of the human world made by millions of people at once. By looking at the artifacts we make, we have the opportunity to learn about how we perceive the world.

Nous

Nous (pronounced like news) intends to make the experience of reading the news would be a little less distracting, ugly, and infuriating. Tell it what news source you'd like to read and it will grab the stories from the front page and run them through a machine learning algorithm that makes a guess about the sentiment, or emotional content of a story. It then renders that emotional content as a color, giving you the ability to decide whether or not you'd like to choose to read the story. ***Due to API Query Limits, I can't yet launch a live Demo. Message me if you'd like to try it out ***
